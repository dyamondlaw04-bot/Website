Ethical Issue 1: Protecting Private Information

One ethical issue that could arise in cybersecurity is protecting private information. A Cybersecurity Analyst may have access to sensitive data such as passwords, financial records, employee information, or customer records. This creates an ethical responsibility to only use that information for professional and legal purposes. Misusing private information could violate trust, harm individuals, and damage the organization.

My values of integrity, respect, and responsibility would shape how I handle this issue. Integrity would remind me to do the right thing even if I had access to information others could not see. Respect would remind me that people deserve privacy. Responsibility would push me to follow security policies, protect data, and report risks instead of ignoring them.

Ethical Issue 2: AI Being Used for Cyberattacks

Another ethical issue is the use of Artificial Intelligence in cyberattacks. AI can be used to create phishing emails, fake messages, deepfakes, or automated attacks that are harder to detect. In a cybersecurity career, this creates the challenge of using AI in a responsible way while also defending against people who use AI to harm others.

My values of honesty and responsibility would guide me in this situation. Honesty would keep me from using AI tools in a harmful or misleading way. Responsibility would encourage me to use AI to improve protection, detect threats, and educate others about online risks. As a future Cybersecurity Analyst, I would want to use technology to protect people rather than take advantage of them.

Ethical Issue 3: Bias in AI Security Tools

A third ethical issue is bias in AI security tools. Some AI systems may make unfair decisions if they are trained on incomplete or biased data. In cybersecurity, this could lead to certain users, groups, or behaviors being flagged unfairly. This matters because technology should not create unfair treatment or false accusations.

My values of respect, honesty, and integrity would help me handle this issue. Respect would remind me to think about how technology affects real people. Honesty would encourage me to report problems with AI tools instead of pretending they are perfect. Integrity would push me to support fair and accurate cybersecurity practices.
